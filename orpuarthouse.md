## Product vision & phases
- **Phase 1 (now):** marketing landing page that converts visitors to students via Instagram DM.
- **Phase 2 (when class structure + videos are ready):** the same site becomes a paid online
  class platform — students **choose the topics** they want, **pay according to region**
  (India + Western markets), and watch **gated video lessons**. Phase 1 is built so Phase 2
  bolts on without a rebuild.

## Who she is
**Poojitha** — fashion illustrator, working under **Orpu Art House** (@orpuarthouse).

illustration. Account is early-stage, so the page must build trust on its own.

**Positioning:** *Learn to bring fashion to life on paper.* Taught by a working illustrator,
beginner-friendly.

## Audience
Aspiring illustrators, fashion-design students, and serious hobbyists — in India and the West —
who want to draw garments and figures. They convert when they see (a) skill, (b) that it's
*teachable* in steps, and (c) a topic/track that fits their level. Biggest fear to defuse:
"I can't draw well enough to start."

## Brand voice & visual direction
- **Editorial, gallery-like, calm.** Lots of white/off-white space, charcoal text.
- **Accent color = the red** from her signature gown work. Use sparingly.
- Elegant **serif** headers; clean sans body. The **artwork is the only loud element.**
- Copy tone: warm, encouraging, confident; not salesy.

## Content tracks (the spine — and the future "topics")
1. **Fashion croquis / figure sketching** (graphite) — fundamentals.
2. **Garment rendering** (colored pencil) — her strongest, most sellable work. HERO material.
3. **Ethnic / Indian-wear illustration** — the market differentiator most teachers skip.
Plus a minor **"Beyond fashion"** strip (portrait, still-life) to show range.
These three tracks are the natural basis for the Phase-2 purchasable topics.

## Video strategy
- **Now (Phase 1):** the 19 clips are **free marketing/teaser footage** (process time-lapses).
  Muted on-page loops + click-to-play flagships. Unlisted YouTube acceptable for teasers only.
- **Later (Phase 2):** actual paid lessons are separate, gated, and hosted on a signed-URL
  service — never unlisted YouTube. See `phase2-architecture.md`.

## Hero strategy
- **Red draped gown = the signature.** Lead with it (muted loop + flagship process video).
- **Ethnic wear = the differentiator.** Feature the full ethnic render prominently.
- **Before → After** (sketch → render) is the single most persuasive proof of teachability.

## Landing page sections (v1 order)
1. Header / nav — wordmark + "Message on Instagram"
2. Hero — muted red-gown loop, headline, DM CTA
3. Signature "watch it come alive" — flagship click-to-play process video
4. Before → After — sketch-to-render proof
5. What you'll learn — three tracks as tiles w/ short clips (+ offer/topics placeholder)
6. Portfolio gallery — finished pieces, optional bucket filter

8. Beyond fashion — small range strip

10. FAQ — defuse beginner fears (do I need to draw already? materials? online? time?)
11. Final CTA band — "Ready to start? Message me 'CLASSES'"
12. Footer — IG link, wordmark, copyright
